
     <p><b>PIPES Act of 2019</b></p> <p>This bill addresses pipeline safety and infrastructure. It authorizes appropriations through FY2023 for specified pipeline safety programs under the Pipeline Safety Improvement Act of 2002 and related enactments. </p> <p>Among other things, the bill </p> <ul> <li>requires fees for compliance reviews of liquefied natural gas (LNG) facilities;</li> <li>authorizes the establishment of limited safety-enhancing testing programs to evaluate innovative technologies and operational practices testing the safe operation of natural gas or hazardous liquid pipeline facilities;</li> <li>sets forth requirements for idled natural or other gas transmission and hazardous liquid pipelines;</li> <li>updates the standards for LNG facilities;</li> <li>establishes the National Center of Excellence for Liquefied Natural Gas Safety and Training to include a repository of information on best practices relating to LNG operations;</li> <li>requires each distribution integrity plan developed by an operator of a gas distribution pipeline to include the risks from the presence of cast iron pipes and mains and risks that may result from the operation of a low-pressure distribution system; </li> <li>directs operators to establish protocols for communicating with fire, police, and other relevant public officials after a disaster that includes fires, explosions, or one or more fatalities; and</li> <li>requires procedural manuals for operations, maintenance, and emergencies to include certain written procedures.</li> </ul> <ul> </ul>
