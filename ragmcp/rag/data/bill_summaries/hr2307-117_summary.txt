
     <p><b>Energy Innovation and Carbon Dividend Act of 2021</b></p> <p>This bill imposes a fee on the carbon content of fuels, including crude oil, natural gas, coal, or any other product derived from those fuels that will be used so as to emit greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. </p> <p>The fee is imposed on the producers or importers of the fuels and is equal to the greenhouse gas content of the fuel multiplied by the carbon fee rate. The rate begins at $15 per metric ton of CO2-e in 2021, increases by $10 each year, and is subject to further adjustments based on the progress in meeting specified emissions reduction targets. </p> <p>The bill includes</p> <ul> <li> exemptions for fuels used for agricultural or nonemitting purposes,</li> <li>exemptions for fuels used by the Armed Forces,</li> <li> rebates for facilities that capture and sequester carbon dioxide, and</li> <li>border adjustment provisions that require certain fees or refunds for carbon-intensive products that are exported or imported. </li> </ul> <p>The fees must be deposited into a Carbon Dividend Trust Fund and used for administrative expenses and dividend payments to U.S. citizens or lawful residents. The fees must be decommissioned when emissions levels and monthly dividend payments fall below specified levels.</p>
